1. Introduction

This assignment studies an AI scheduling problem where a set of assignments must be completed under prerequisite constraints, group size constraint (g students per day), fixed daily food menu constraint, and the goal of minimizing total food cost.

Each assignment requires a specific food item based on difficulty level:

TC - Tandoori Chicken
PM - Paneer Butter Masala
DF - Dal Fry
GJ - Gulab Jamun

Students can solve at most g assignments per day, and only one menu can be ordered per day.

Thus, the problem becomes a constrained optimization problem combining:

• Topological scheduling
• Resource allocation
• Cost optimization

We implemented:

a. Greedy algorithms (approximate solution)
b. A* search algorithm (optimal solution)

--------------------------------------------------

2. Problem Representation

Each assignment is represented as:

A = (id, prerequisites, output, food)

Example:

A5 depends on outputs of A1 and A2.

The structure forms a Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG).

Goal:
Schedule all assignments such that:

1. dependencies are satisfied
2. no more than g assignments are done per day
3. total food cost is minimized

--------------------------------------------------

3. Greedy Strategies

Greedy algorithms make locally optimal choices at each step.

We implemented two greedy strategies.

--------------------------------------------------

3.1 Greedy by Food Cost

Strategy:

Select assignments requiring the cheapest food item first.

Motivation:

Lower food prices reduce daily cost.

Selection rule:

Choose up to g available assignments with minimum food cost.

Advantages:

• simple
• fast
• reduces expensive menu items early

Disadvantages:

• ignores dependency structure
• may increase total days

--------------------------------------------------

3.2 Greedy by Food Frequency

Strategy:

Prioritize assignments whose food type appears most frequently among remaining assignments.

Motivation:

If many assignments require TC, scheduling them together reduces menu diversity.

Selection rule:

Choose assignments with most frequent food type.

Advantages:

• reduces menu variation
• increases chance of shared menu

Disadvantages:

• may delay critical assignments
• not always optimal

--------------------------------------------------

4. A* Search Algorithm

A* guarantees optimal solution using:

f(n) = g(n) + h(n)

where:

g(n) = cost accumulated so far
h(n) = estimated future cost

--------------------------------------------------

4.1 State Representation

Each state contains:

• completed assignments
• daily schedule
• total cost so far

--------------------------------------------------

4.2 Cost Function

Daily cost is computed as:

sum of food costs in menu

Example:

Menu = {2 TC, 1 PM}

Cost = 2×TC + 1×PM

--------------------------------------------------

4.3 Heuristic Function

Heuristic estimates minimum possible future cost.

We used:

sum of cheapest food costs of remaining assignments

Properties:

• admissible
• never overestimates
• guarantees optimal solution

8. Analysis

Observations:

1. Greedy approaches performed well because food costs were similar.
2. A* explored many states but confirmed optimal cost.
3. A* sometimes required more days because it optimizes cost only.
4. Group size significantly impacts schedule efficiency.
5. Dependency structure affects availability of assignments.

Key insight:

Food cost minimization does not always minimize number of days.

Trade-off exists between:

• schedule length
• menu consistency

--------------------------------------------------

9. Conclusion

We implemented Greedy and A* algorithms to solve constrained scheduling.

Findings:

Greedy methods provide fast approximate solutions.
A* guarantees optimal food cost.
In our test cases, Greedy achieved near-optimal results.

A* is recommended when:

optimal cost is critical.

Greedy is recommended when:

fast scheduling is required.
